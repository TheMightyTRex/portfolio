About me

Clarity is a design decision.

I'm a content designer with a deep specialism in training design, with over two decades spent turning legislation, product complexity, and process change into learning content that genuinely helps people.

My career at IRIS Software Group spanned front-line support, senior consulting, CPD-accredited training delivery, and a move into full content design — giving me a rare end-to-end perspective on what users actually need and why they struggle.

I believe great training isn't about dumping information — it's about structuring it so people can act on it. I bring instructional design rigour, content design sensibility, and genuine curiosity to every project.

"Known for being approachable, patient, and great at breaking down complex information — I thrive in environments where communication, teamwork, and adaptability matter."

How I work

My design process

01
Discover

Understand user needs, support trends, analytics, and SME input to identify the real problem before touching any content.

02
Define

Map learning objectives, audience personas, and scope. Decide format: module, video, help article, or blended programme.

03
Design

Structure content using instructional design principles. Storyboard, script, and prototype — testing clarity at every step.

04
Develop

Build in Articulate Storyline, MadCap Flare, Camtasia, or Teachable — applying accessibility standards and brand consistency throughout.

05
Measure

Track impact via analytics, NPS, support ticket reduction, and learner feedback. Iterate continuously to maintain relevance.

✦ Users first, always

Every content decision starts with the question: will this actually help the user do what they're trying to do? Not what the business wants to say — what the user needs to know.

✦ Evidence over assumption

Support data, analytics, and direct feedback drive content priorities. I don't redesign content because it looks old — I redesign it because evidence shows it's failing users.

✦ Accessibility by design

WCAG 2.1 compliance and inclusive language aren't a checklist at the end of a project — they're embedded from the first draft. All formats, all audiences.
